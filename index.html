What is the Re-Engagement Center Network?

A new ballot measure that will create an Independent Redistricting Commission for Oakland.

Collaborating with Public Systems

Every ten years, the U.S. census counts every person in the country. Based on this new count of where populations are and what has changed, Oakland’s political district lines for local elections are redrawn so that all districts have nearly the same amount of people. Under this new measure an independent commission of Oakland residents forms to draw Oakland’s seven geographical council districts for the election of council and school board seats.

Key Components

Up until now these lines have been drawn by politicians. But Measure DD puts the power back into the hand of Oakland residents. This commission is required to be "reflective of the geographic, racial, ethnic and economic diversity of the City of Oakland." That means that the people who serve on the Independent Redistricting Commission will come from different neighborhoods, different backgrounds, and different experiences to ensure that the diversity of Oakland is reflected in the final map.

Geography and Model Variation

The Independent Redistricting Commission offers many ways for all Oakland residents to be engaged in this important process. Residents will be invited to share their wisdom and experience to guide how district lines are drawn by a commission that is committed to keeping communities of interest together. The commission is required to widely publicize all opportunities for residents to participate to ensure broad participation from diverse communities.

Why Focus on Opportunity Youth?

Power to make new districts should reside in the hands of the people.

Removes political self interest

We think that everyday Oaklanders should be at the forefront of determining our districts, because we know the ins and outs of our neighborhoods and our communities. When residents make the decisions instead of politicians, we can keep our neighborhoods and our people together to flex our political power and win policies and elect politicians that improve our quality of life.

Cost Neutral

There is no additional cost to the City for the work of this new commission. This commission’s budget will be the same as the legally required redistricting process cost in 2013, adjusted for inflation. This amount will be set aside in the 10 years before each redistricting process happens.
